CONTRACTS

Upon hearing of the new pilot, several parties have taken the opportunity to offer you beginner contracts, and are willing to pay up-front.

  • You may only choose one tier per contract, but the rewards do not acculumate from lower tiers
  • You must be able perform the lower tier requirements in addition to the one you're taking as well
  • Rewards that aren't credits are awarded after completing the contract

MAIN CAMPAIGN: THE HARBINGER WAR

Mind not the squirrel on my shoulder, I assure you he is no Gazer spy. Forgive me for the cryptic introductions; I am Aarvold, the grand architect behind the Apocalypse. War is upon us whether we wished for it or not, and I hope you bring your full might to bear for the events to come. The theatres have been set, and you may choose which lead to follow.

THEATRE OF FIRE

Tier 1

Forty years ago, a great coalition of pirates was formed. Suppressed in the outskirts too long by Blackhawk Elite mercenaries, groups of all manner banded together to drive them out in a series of bloody struggles. As a result, they tore a hole through Blackhawk-controlled space, parting it in two to make way for the inexorable storm.

The system of Mjölnir was one of the Blackhawk Elite's major staging systems, but it folded to the coalition in a matter of weeks. With the help of the influential Luos Syndicate's Core Ring, it was handed over to the Fire Legion-a large alliance of bandits which spearheaded the assault. There, they barricaded themselves in and then inexplicably severed contact with their former allies. The afterglow of war held its embrace heavy on everyone, so nobody thought anything of it.

Decades passed by, and turmoil in other regions gradually drew away the scrutiny of the frontier power blocs. Little did they know that in the ruins of the planets of Mjölnir, an anomalous chaos was festering. What was once the Fire Legion became something otherworldly. Elevated, fanatical, and rabid.

In recent years, the Legion has made an appearance again. Small gangs wandered away from their impregna- ble territory, attacking anyone they happened upon indiscriminately. These skirmishes gradually grew in frequency and size, until suddenly, all hell broke loose.

Tier 2

Like an army of ancient gods on their chariots descending from the heav- ens, a great fleet emerged from the entropic mists. At first it engulfed Blackhawk ship patrols and roaming Red Dagger gangs, but eventually it was smashing through fortified blockades and bases. It took the combined efforts of the Blackhawks, Talons and Federation Navy to put a stop to the fleet, but not soon after, another Legion armada emerged.

For the first time, the world saw the effectiveness of the agents of the Apocalypse. The original fireteam, consisting of the agents Archangel, Locust, Plague and Styx, assembled among the unified forces. In the ensuing Battle of Carceri, they played a pivotal role and turned the tide. But still, today, the Fire Legion continues to push forward, seemingly blinded by a delirious fervor with no loss of momentum.

Tier 3

Around the war table sits a top com- mander from every involved faction. Assets and logistics are constantly being juggled and despite their differ- ences (and for some, hatred) of each other, a valiant effort is being put together. Differences in doctrines be damned, everyone agrees that a push needs be made to Mjölnir. An admiral from the Federation Navy even proposed to start a propaganda campaign for funds.

However, it is becoming increasingly apparent that the projection and flexibility of all available fleets is not enough. The Fire Legion has seemingly no limit in their reinforcements, their scope encompasses no meaningful pattern, and all attempts at negotiation are met with garbled comms and white noise. It has become clear that the Fire Legion are no longer a known force, but instead pawns thralled to an incongruous enemy.

All eyes beseech the Apocalypse program to offer a new element to this war.

Tier 4

The first Legion fleets encountered were simply scraps of machinery cobbled together from industrial ships and hulls salvaged from abandoned mercenary ves- sels. Now, in more recent fleets, ships are emitting bizarre energy signatures akin to that of mana, though with a more volatile and unstable wavelength.

Radio analysis from covert-ops scouts returns disturbing intel. Planets and moons in Mjölnir and her surrounding systems have seen a surge in geothermal activity- something concurrently spurs these satellites in a manner never before seen to mankind. Bewilderment strikes all experts and theorists at arms, but whatever is happening there indicates productivity-an industrialization of planetary resources at the scale of a galactic neighborhood. Mjölnir and her sister systems have been turned into factories of war.

It's only a matter of time before we witness what abominable horrors will flood out from those worlds. Our only ambition now is to push back the tide, and we can only hope that even in victory, we won't fold in to whatever ungodly mechanisms await our discovery.

DISPUTE OF REALITIES

Tier 1

With the emergence of hell taking the world by storm, enemies outside the focus of battle have been flittering to and fro. Puppets that they are, the people who were once the Fire Legion are not the only ones among their ranks. There is no longer any doubt that this is all the work of a pernicious, exotic force.

K26L-P is a remote system just outside of Fire Legion-controlled space, and is just out of the way of the Blackhawk Elite's sovereignty at the region of Valhalla. Here, Dustkeeper explorers alongside agents working with the Gazers have discovered an anomaly on the moon of K26L-P IX, dubbed the "Scorched Cliffs" due to the violent storms that can form in the atmosphere. Temporary bases have been set up, and recent events have sparked top-level interest from all of the major power blocs.

Tier 2

Jutting out from a tall mountain is a monolithic structure that leads down into the moon. Geological analysis returns the possibility that it goes down to the very core, and the surrounding rock formations suggests that the moon may have formed around it. This abnormality baffles Dustkeeper scientists, since that would imply that the structure precedes any known Herald artifact by billions of years.

The monolith was not undefended when the Dustkeepers arrived. What appeared to be automatons wielding mana-like weaponry were assembled around the Scorched Cliffs, patrolling. They were dealt with after heavy losses and reinforcements, but the absurd similarty between their corps- es implies mass production, perhaps even some form of duplication. They were tough with almost impenetrable shields, and yet it's reasonable to believe that these were less than footsoldiers among their ranks. Sightings and video recordings of the skirmishes conclude another issue, however. Among the automatons were several other animated beings clad in different look- ing armour.

Tier 3

When the Gazers arrived, they confirmed the sightings with tales of the existence of an extraordinary race of beings. Given the shady history of the Gazers, others can only choose to believe their stories.

They are the Harbingers. They are the hands which moves the invasion, and their goal is the unmitigated extermi- nation of their enemies.

The Gazers released a manifesto containing documents and logs from a time long past, detailing an exalted alien race who con from a different plane. They wield unusual and unorthadox technology, and are thereby masters of mana.

The documents depict that the Harbingers are divided up into clans, each with different beliefs and practices. And each clan aligns themselves to one of two alliances-one alliance with an aspect for the divine, and the other with an aspect over the cosmos. They are not on friendly terms with each other, but something was profound enough to unify them towards a single cause-an invasion of our world.

It is unknown why the moon was so lightly defended, but with the Scorched Cliffs now occupied by allied forces, it's only a matter of time before an assault force is sent to take it back. Even with a Blackhawk Elite blockade and reinforcements on the way, the monolith must be defended at all costs.

Tier 4

Deep down beneath the monolith is a facility that houses a spatial anomaly-a wormhole contained by an accretion disk of mana. We know that this is what the Harbingers came to the moon for, but to where it leads is not initially apparent. Probes sent in either don't return results or don't return at all. Gazer squads have supposedly gone in without notice of others, but several Apocalypse fireteams have volunteered to enter as well.

The Alliance of the Divine is the one you must be wary of. They hold power over the Alliance of the Cosmos who are little more than subjects in their council. An old conflict between the two had sparked but was brief, and was decisevly ended with the slaughtering of many of their own.

I theorize that the other side of this wormhole is where that conflict took place. In other words, it is a portal to their own world, one similar to the one which the Fire Legion may have discovered on Mjölnir. There is no doubt that the Harbingers on the other side will meet you with violent accord, but if we're to turn the tide of this war, we can start with a foothold in their home.

THE CULT OF ALD

Tier 1

Far into the edges of the frontier lies the last vestiges of lawlessness. Some deem it a realm for savages, seen only in the light of civillization during the rare times a stray Blackhawk patrol wanders past. But others call it the last bastion for liberty and freedom, and the Red Dagger Pirates call it home.

In the wake of the war against the Luos Syndicate, mass disarray has led the powers that be to find a lull in their aggressive strategies as they retreated to reor- ganize. Thus, many pirate crews found themselves a world aplenty with salvage and bounty, but some saw the calm as a bad omen. Star sailors have their superstitions, but none are more proficient at it than a pirate.

Whispers of dark arts at work spread through the Red Dagger alliance like wildfire. Prominent Dagger veterans who recently scoured the edges of the map speak of robed heretics and strange beasts that hide there. Grainy, unfamiliar recordings have been passed around only between the most trusted and seasoned captains. In a desert warped with crags and sand, temples stood with spires that scraped the heavens. From a tomb bathed in cerulean light, a thousand eyes dazzled like a bed of stars. And in a place describable only as the darkest corridor of time, a great shadow with ivory choirs for teeth sung into the abyss.

For the third time in recent memory, a pirate council was called, and all the free people of the Red Daggers gathered for their greatest foe yet.

Tier 2

The Cult of Ald has been a thorn in the side of many different groups for many long decades, but neither did their cryptic messages or savage symbolism give light to their purpose. Human experimentation is a popular conclusion to the Cult's exploits, and inexplainable encounters with the few cultists found stranded in civiliza- tion only helped highlight the unnatu- rality of their body movements.

It was as though they were human no more. They moved in ways no human beings could ever without augmentation, and some claim that they could even jump up to tall buildings. Yet, no electronic emissions or digital signatures have ever been detectable or traceable among them. Forboding yet is the increase in their activity in recent years. With the incursion of the Fire Legion escalating to horrific meas- ures, the Cult of Ald appears to be stirring in its wake. Captured images of cultist groups roaming planets where nobody should be have been among the surveillance logs distributed around the Red Daggers.

Tier 3

Talon spies working closely with Red Daggers bear intel of the sightings of several temple-like structures, seem- ingly appearing on random planets without ever being detected before. Analytics of intel data recovered suggests that the Cult's movements surround these temples as though they are an epicenter. Some pirate crews have even been attacked by broadsides that seemed to come out of nowhere when sailing through these areas, and before a culprit could ever be detected, the threat vanishes into thin air.

Seven temples have been found thus far, but the scouts are confident that there are more of them out there.

Tier 4

The Cult of Ald... named as such after the constellation of Ald's Crown-a distant region of space explored decades ago by deep space explorers from Pulsar Inc. It's a place plentiful with Herald technology. Extensive abandoned structures orbit moons, and fleets of Herald ships lie dormant in hangars spanning several planets. That expedition is listed as a catastrophic failure in official logs, for they were never heard from again. But indeed, they have returned as the foes you fight now.

old stories tell of an order of knights dedicated to the fight against the paranormal, ages past. They hunted tyrant gods with weapons of sapphire, blue as the sea and deep as the abyss. But as they fought to cleanse the world from evil, they perished by their own weapons. One by one, their souls were consumed and replaced by the essence of the very things they sought to annihilate.

Through all that I've witnessed in this vast universe, there are few things that still make me tremble. The Cult has harnessed the power of mana in ways I could not dare to fathom, and perhaps there are secrets that the Heralds uncovered which lead them to their doom.
